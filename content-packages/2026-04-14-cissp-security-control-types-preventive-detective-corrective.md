# LinkedIn post

**Security Controls: More Than Just Tools — How to Think Like a CISSP**

Most teams can name a few security controls. Fewer can explain why we group them as preventive, detective, corrective, deterrent, or compensating—or how those types work together to reduce real risk.

If you’re studying for CISSP (or building a security program), here’s the mindset shift:

- Preventive controls block incidents before they start (think firewalls, access controls)
- Detective controls tell you when something’s wrong (logs, IDS, monitoring)
- Corrective controls help you recover (backups, incident response)
- Deterrent and compensating controls fill the gaps

The real power comes from layering these types. No single control is perfect, but together they make your defenses resilient.

How do you map your controls? Where are your gaps?
